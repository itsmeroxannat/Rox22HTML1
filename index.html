Bellisima Roma

Known as "The Eternal City"

-Romes first University, La Sapienza, established 1303 AD, is the largest in Europe and the second largest in the world.
One of the most prestigious univeristy that taught many Nobel prize winners, famous scientist and politicians.

-There are more than 2000 fountains in Rome.
More than 1.3 million euros is tossed every year in the most famous fountain, Fontana Trevi. Thats about 3,561 euros per day.

-Cats are protected by the law, they can live wherever they please.

Amalfi Coast

-According to Greek Mythology, Poseidon created the village of Positano to show his love to Pasithea, the goddess of rest and relaxation.


-Lemons are the unofficial symbol of the Amalfi Coast. Known as Sfusato Amalfitano; use of the whole lemon - down to the last drop! The most famous product made out of lemons is probably the Limeoncello.


-The Amalfi Coast was recently featured in the film Wonder Woman as the fictional island of Themyscira, the birthplace of Wonder Woman.
